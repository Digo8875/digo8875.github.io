The Blog

As we live, we decide which paths we want to follow. For my professional path, i chose the area of Technology.

I have always sought to learn and have never had any problems sharing my knowledge with others.
Since high school, my friends would come to me for help with their subjects, to talk about ideas and to do their work. However, it was in college that I realized this and that I like to share my knowledge to help others.
So, I started to pay more attention to the way I did and presented my work, always aiming to pass on knowledge to others.
I believe that realizing this has also helped me professionally, because whenever someone asks for help, I try to explain it in a way that the person can “really understand” and not just “this is the theory, copy and paste”.

On the other hand, our lives are not just about achievements and good things. I have always been more reserved in my own corner, so exposing something ‘publicly’ is always difficult for me.
I confess that I have thought a few times about making tutorials or a blog to pass on knowledge to people, but we are always afraid of ‘never being good enough’, ‘never having enough knowledge’, ‘will we really help anyone?’.

Our lives are fast-paced and we need to expose ourselves to new experiences to discover more about ourselves and be happy during our time here.
I believe that by sharing knowledge I can become better and help other people, so this blog aims to help us learn from each other and evolve through technical conversations, tutorials and ideas.

The Author

It’s a pleasure to meet you. I’m Rodrigo, a Software Developer. I’m from a small town in the south of Minas Gerais, Brazil, and I’ve always lived here.

My contact with technology began in my childhood: I watched my father fix electrical and electronic equipment; I got a computer from my uncles; and I learned how to maintain my computer together with my cousins.

I always played video games with my friends, especially strategy games, because I liked to analyze problems and make decisions. We often thought: ‘how does it work?’, ‘how can someone create this?’, until at a certain point we created some servers for ourselves and edited game files through tutorials. Old times playing Tibia servers.

Later, when I was in high school, an opportunity arose to study Industrial Electronics Technician. At first, I started the course out of curiosity to learn more about the area my father worked in, but it was also here that I came into contact with programming, specifically programming for embedded software, using C language and microcontrollers.

I believe that it was each little ‘coincidence’ that appeared in these experiences that led me to programming.
After completing my Electronics Technician degree, I was still thinking about ‘what else is there in programming’? That’s when I decided to pursue this area and returned to the same institution, now to study Informatic Technician.
I later graduated in Information Systems and currently, in DEC/2024, I have started new challenges, with an MBA in FullCycle Architecture.

I am a professional focused on Backend and I intend to reach a point where I can work in all stages of software development, from design, development, implementation, monitoring and resilience.

I believe that IT is a challenging area, which will always force us to study and adapt to new tools, new technologies, new ways of working, and this awakens an interesting curiosity in me. I believe that I have found myself professionally and I intend to continue in the area for several years.

For the professional part, if you want more information and experiences, access my social links in the side menu, such as GitHub and Linkedin. I also leave a link to my online resume if you want to take a look: Rodrigo Cândido.

“This is the way”

Some of you will recognize this phrase from the series “The Mandalorian”.
So this topic brings some personal ideas that I try to follow on my path in life.

“If you take everything away from you: your money, belongings, achievements, dreams… what is really left in your essence?”
For me, my answer has always been just one word: “Evolution”.

I believe that our existence is based on this ‘evolution’, on the idea of ​​’always becoming better’, regardless of the area of ​​our life: mind, body, beliefs, social life, relationships, finances, etc.
So, for this evolution to be growing and also give us purpose, I imagine there are 3 stages:

  • The basis of everything, Evolving yourself, self-improvement in all aspects;
  • To then be able to help others and Evolve those close to you;
  • Enabling that together with other people we can then Evolve the environment around us;

To do this, we must evaluate ourselves from time to time, see our strengths and weaknesses, allowing us to see what we can improve and act on it.

I believe that we will always have problems in our lives and that each experience will bring us points to analyze, overcome and thus enable us to be better.
Some things I think about this idea:

  • Just complaining is useless. In addition to overloading the people around you, your problems will not be solved. Focus on solving problems and if necessary ask for help to solve them, also focusing on bringing evolution to those involved;
  • We are not machines, take care of your health as a whole. Always analyze yourself and seek your physical and mental balance;
  • In some aspects we will be more resistant to evolving, because our comfort zone is getting in the way. Everything has its time and will depend on our persistence;
  • Rest in your comfort zone but remember that evolution is outside of it;
  • Some people/environments will choose to never evolve. That’s okay, but don’t let that affect you negatively;
  • Evolution is a lifestyle and should be desired, never imposed;
